“Every card bought is an encouragement.” - Galio, printmaker

Greeting cards are beautiful when they're meaningful. That's why Yellowbird Cards aren't mass produced. Instead, every card comes with a story about it's maker. We believe it is those stories - not the automated print press - that make our cards beautiful.

Meet our Printmakers

Meet our Printmakers

Yellowbird travels to developing countries to find and employ workers who were previously under-employed. Yellowbird printmakers receive a living wage, on-the-job skills training, and financial literacy training where they're coached to save funds for future life goals.

Ken

Born in Bercy, Ken supports himself with occasional work making bracelets for tourists. Ken's parents have very limited means, and as a result were only able to afford to send him to elementary school. Ken hopes that as a full-time printmaker he would be able to afford to return to school and support his family.

Galio

A resident of Bercy since 2002, Galio works part-time as a translator for English speaking visitors in Haiti. In his spare time Galio hopes to print cards to help earn extra income, which he plans to use to fund his college education.

Eveline

Eveline resides in Bercy with her children. She hopes to use cardmaking as a way of supporting herself and her family.

Jimmy

For the past 16 years Jimmy has called Bercy home. A wood maker, Jimmy has also been making traditional Haitian cards for several years. Jimmy lives with his parents and one daughter and is the primary breadwinner the family as his father is disabled.
